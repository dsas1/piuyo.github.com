Finding the Players Notes

Finding the Players Summary

A leader sometimes finds herself faced with the need to staff a team in a hurry. People with data science-related skills are in high demand, so be creative and improvise. Some areas you might consider include:

  • Individual Contractors
  • Generalist consultancies (tend to be expensive)
  • High-end boutique (specialist) consultancies
  • Functional-specific consultancies
  • Off-shore consultants
  • MLaaS: Machine Learning as a Service
  • Exploit company rotation programs
  • Technical recruiters
  • University/Boot Camp partnerships
  • Be sure to look inside your organization there may be hidden gems

ND045 DSBL L03 Finding The Players Walkthrough

Finding the Players Walkthrough Summary

When I joined Sears as Chief Data Scientist, the first time I met the CEO he told me “when you come to me, I want to talk about opportunities that are at least a $100 million, margin accretive”. At the time, my team consisted of the one remaining junior data scientist from a converted Economic Modeling team, and one member of Sears’ management rotation program, a recent MBA grad whose current rotation was with my data science team.
I needed to scale up fast and began looking for people to join the team. Of course, the market was tight for these highly technical specialists, so I took full advantage of my network to find talent quickly.
In addition to traditional channels, I found talent in the following places:

  • A special data science consultancy provided several people.
  • I brought in two more people who were on MBA rotational programs through Sears.
  • I hired one person with an MBA in business analytics as an intern
  • I brought in 3 contractors that I had worked with previously on my Penlink team,
  • And I filled in technical gaps with team members from an offshore data science consultancy.

As you can see, I had to get quite creative to fill out the project with the team members needed.

Data Science Hackers

  • The Data Science Hacker is a generalist, experienced with many of the technologies required for digitally transformative projects.
  • The Data Science Hacker is literate in multiple specialties and will help you scope out your project with their inquisitive minds and understanding of the big picture.
  • Data Science Hackers apply and combine established packages and open-source examples to get a proof of concept in minimal time, work with the business, interact with end-users, and perform presentations.
