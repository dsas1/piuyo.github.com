Developing Your Intuition About Human Capital of Data Science Summary

In this lesson, we will consider the organizational and talent issues that surround building a data science practice. Although data science human capital issues share many characteristics of building teams and organizations in other technical areas, the distinct mix of skills and job requirements of a data scientist introduces several considerations unique to data science.

One of the particular challenges arises in sourcing data scientist candidates.

As we proceed through this lesson, we will cover strategies specifically designed to meet the challenges of staffing and managing a data science practice.

True or False: The talent and organizational challenges of building a data science practice closely mirror those of building other technology or engineering teams.
SOLUTION: False

Reflect

QUESTION:

Name three educational degree fields that serve as common backgrounds for data scientists.

ANSWER:

Data scientists can come from virtually any field -- one of my most talented data scientists was a music theory major! Common backgrounds include Computer Science, Mathematics, Statistics, Economics, Physics, and any other field that has a quantitative component to it.
