ND045 DSBL L03 Configuring The Team Walkthrough

Configuring the Team Walkthrough Summary

Let's walk through an example of how to configure a team, and plan for the future, using the data science heat map. This particular example comes from one of the teams that I built for a medium-sized organization that was just starting out on its data science journey. Recall that the data science heat map specifies individuals involved in data science initiatives according to their specific roles, their levels of experience, and expertise.

In year one, my priority was building prototypes and delivering on projects that gave quick wins in order to build momentum for the data science practice.
I knew that I needed the most experience and expertise in my data science roles. As such, I filled two of my open FTEs with data scientists, one a master data scientist who could lead many of the efforts of the team, and a second fellow data scientist who was able to independently lead many aspects of the initial prototypes. Our prototypes were embedded in machine learning, so I needed a skilled machine learning engineer who I hired at the fellow level.
We built our data science products projects into internally focused products. So fellow-level professionals in data visualization and software engineering also played key roles on the first-year team. In this early stage, I did not employ any additional adjunct roles, but I did have as stakeholders the CEO, the chief data officer, and two functional heads, the chief marketing officer and the chief technological officer.

In year two, I built on the momentum and enthusiasm that we had generated in year one to better fill out the team for developing and hosting models in production. As such, I added two members to the team. The first was a fellow-level data engineer who could build professional-grade ETL pipelines to bring data from disparate silos throughout the organization directly to the point of analytics. I also added a full-stack engineer at the fellow level to the team, whose role was critical in building production-quality applications with embedded data science.

Year three marked a major build-out of our Data Science footprint, mainly through adjunct roles to support our work that was now in production.
I added three apprentice-level engineers to the team - one a full-stack engineer, a second who specialized in front end engineering, and a third who specialized in back end engineering. I also added a fellow level UI/UX engineer, as we were now working closely with customers to get feedback that informed iterations of the product. As the team's operational scope grew more expansive, I added a fellow level Product Manager to manage activities. Finally, as our product became open to external customers accessing the product through a web interface, I contracted with a security engineer who was able to address critical issues around maintaining the security of our product.
