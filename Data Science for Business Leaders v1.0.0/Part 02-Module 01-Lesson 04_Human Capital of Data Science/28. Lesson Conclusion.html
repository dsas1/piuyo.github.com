Lesson Conclusion Summary

We have come to the end of our lesson on the human capital of data science.

Here is what we have covered:

Organizing People:

  • Organizational models, and how the data science organization might be organized within the company.
  • Identifying stakeholders and building momentum, as well as building the bench of data science professionals.
  • How to find the right people for your data science team, and how to configure that team, using the data science heat map.

Once we have the people on board we need to have a process by which they carry out data science projects. To this end we discussed

  • How product management frameworks such as Agile, Waterfall, and Wagile will help structure your day to day activities as you execute a data science project.
  • Implementing tools for structured problem solving
  • Applying Mintos’s and Schramm’s frameworks for synthesizing with actionable insights and storytelling with data.
  • Implementing strategies for fostering a data-driven culture, something that is key to an organization's ability to truly become data-driven.

In the next lesson, we will discuss the technological infrastructure that needs to be put in place to support data science initiatives. I look forward to seeing you there!
