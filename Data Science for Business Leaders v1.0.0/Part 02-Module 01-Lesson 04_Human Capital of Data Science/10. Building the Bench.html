ND045 DSBL L03 Building The Bench Walkthrough

Building the Bench Walkthrough Summary

Executive leadership has allocated eight full-time professional roles for a new data science organization. How might we organize these roles into teams within the organization?
I would organize my professionals into three groups. First, I would field two project-specific teams to be deployed on individual projects on the roadmap.
I would also keep a third group of professionals who, at this stage, would float between the two teams.

My project teams would heavy on data scientists, as the skill set associated with that role tends to be able to complete many of the tasks critical on the road to developing prototypes and proofs of concept. I would not hire only generalists, however -- a machine learning engineer would be a valuable specialist for teams developing data science projects for which machine learning is key.

I would fill my last two positions with a Data Engineer and a Data Visualization Engineer. At this point the goal is to develop working prototypes, so I believe that data engineering and data visualization skills will not yet be in demand enough to allocate full-time specialists in those areas to individual teams. Once our prototypes prove the concept and value of a data science solution, they will be built into production-quality products and applications, at which point my specialist talent base will need to become much more deep.

Project Team 1:

  • Data Scientist
  • Data Scientist
  • Machine Learning Engineer

Project Team 2:

  • Data Scientist
  • Data Scientist
  • Machine Learning Engineer

Float between the two teams:

  • Data Engineer
  • Data Visualization Engineer
