Machine Learning Architectures Summary

Build In House: In many of today's largest companies it is common for the machine learning architecture to be deployed, hosted, and maintained entirely in-house; this was the case for the Big Data Analytics team I built at Seagate, where we hosted all three layers – data, analytics, and user - on our own servers that we maintained. Seagate had specialized expertise in datacenter and cloud deployment, which made hosting internally a natural choice.

Cloud-hosted Architecture: At companies that did not have such a level of skill, I have leveraged public cloud providers for hosting and deployment. I have personally worked with three of the top public cloud providers - Amazon Web Services (AWS), Google Cloud Platform (GCP), and Microsoft Azure. Managed services, such as AWS Sagemaker, provide many of the data architecture, security, and monitoring services necessary for data and machine learning architecture management, providing an alternative well-suited for the organization that does not have the internal expertise to maintain its own solutions in-house.

Commercial Solutions: For organizations with little development expertise, data science is still possible through commercial solutions such as Software as a Service or Machine Learning as a Service provider. For these companies, an off-the-shelf solution, such as those of Alteryx or SAS, is preferable to building and maintaining the infrastructure to support data science (either internally or via a public cloud). Commercial solutions provide ready-to-go functionality that essentially combines all three layers of the data science architecture. For companies wishing to start out with analytics with targeted analytics and machine learning use cases, who have data science or data analytic talent on hand but lack the data engineering and DevOps skills necessary to build, configure, maintain and support a data science architecture, a commercial alternative can provide a compelling way to access the benefits of data science.

Machine Learning Build vs Buy Summary

If you are building your own architecture, hosted either in-house or via a public cloud, you have quite a lot of flexibility regarding the data storage and computing capabilities you might need. These must be matched to your use case; if you intend to deploy complex deep learning models, for example, you will need a great deal more computing power than if your models tend more toward parametric and tree-based alternatives.

The type of data you intend to use will determine your selection of data storage technology – whether that be in a data lake or in a relational or nonrelational data store. Cloud hosting provides elastic capabilities so that computing and storage can be scaled on-demand, a critical degree of flexibility for many companies whose data science workloads may be uneven or likely to change.

Finally, cloud hosting solutions provide automated services for model monitoring and maintenance, the final key component of a machine learning architecture.
Monitoring should focus particularly on whether model performance degrades over time, as ground truth data drifts from the training set on which the model was trained. Monitoring solutions allow the user to set predefined thresholds and scheduled procedures that identify model drift and trigger retraining and redeploying the model and can provide A/B testing capabilities to support the redeploy decision. Cloud monitoring and maintenance solutions typically integrate with appropriate notification technologies, such as SMS or Slack.

Critical factors in the "build or buy" decision:

  • Size of initiative: In general, smaller initiatives favor purchasing an outside platform, because the fixed costs of in-house development and support are too large for a small project.
  • Complexity of models: Standard models and algorithms favor purchasing a platform since the platform is likely to support most standard approaches.
  • Cash resources: A well-funded budget can favor purchasing an outside platform, as providers charge a premium for hosting and supporting services that do not have to be maintained in-house. Alternatively, smaller budgets might favor cloud providers or more targeted commercial applications, since either avoids the need for large capital outlays required of in-house solutions.
  • Internal technical skills: Lower internal technical skills favor purchasing an external platform, since the organization’s existing team may not have the expertise to develop in-house.
  • Maintenance budget: A smaller long-term maintenance budget favors an external platform since most of the maintenance cost will be borne by the vendor.
