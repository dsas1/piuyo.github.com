Course Recap

We have discussed Data Science, who is a Data Scientist, and what is possible through Data Science. We noted that a critical starting point for any data strategy is the articulation of a business’s strategic objectives and identification of opportunities for data science-based transformation.

We discussed the human capital component of Data Science, which is critical to delivering on a data science strategy. We discussed recruiting, hiring, and training for our Data Science organization. We discussed organizational structure for the data science organization, and we learned how our Data Scientists carry out their work in a structured manner. We also discussed how to leverage data and data science to foster a data-driven culture throughout the business.

Finally, we noted that executing the Data Science strategy requires technology for data and technology for machine learning. Technology needs are specific to each business; they depend on the types of data to be leveraged for Data Science, the form and magnitude of that data, the types of data science models that a business plans to create, and the overall scale of operations represented by those data science models. To that end, we discussed the parameters that must be considered both in creating a Data and Data Architecture Strategy and in building a Machine Learning Architecture to support Data Science initiatives.
