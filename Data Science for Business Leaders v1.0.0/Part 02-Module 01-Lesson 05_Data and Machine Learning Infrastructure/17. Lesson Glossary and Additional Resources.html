Course Glossary

KeyTerm Definition
Agile A project management process that is much more flexible and iterative. Work is prioritized and often completed in short time frames known as "Sprints".
Analysis Layer the machine learning architecture to support analytics and machine learning tasks
Anomaly Detection Methods are used to identify aberrant or unusual cases, based on the collection of variables that characterize those cases.
Artificial Intelligence A class of algorithms that direct machines in activities that mimic the cognition and action of humans.
Centralized Data Science Organization manages all data and data science-related projects centrally through a group that typically includes a chief data officer.
Classification Predicting toward a categorical outcome
Clustering Algorithms is to identify groups of cases according to similarities in their variables.
COGS Cost of Goods Sold
Convenience Some types of data may be easier to collect than other types of data, thus skewing the data you have available in your ground truth dataset, and then skewing your ML model.
Data Analytics the science of analyzing raw data in order to drive business processes and improve business outcomes.
Data Layer the Data and Data Architecture used to bring data to the point of analysis
Data Science A field of study that combines domain expertise, programming skills, and knowledge of mathematics and statistics to extract meaningful insights from data.
Decentralized Data Science Organization Functional areas and business units independently define and manage their data and data science initiatives.
Deep Learning Powerful class of supervised learning methods that underpins many of today's most exciting use cases in artificial intelligence.
Descriptive Approaches require statistical analysis to identify, summarize, and describe a phenomenon of interest.
Dimensionality Reduction is a technique for reducing the complexity of the set of features or variables that might subsequently be used in a supervised learning context.
ETL Extract, Transfer, Load is a process for creating a reliable data pipeline.
Federation The combination of decentralized and centralized management.
KPI Key Performance Indicator used to measure success.
Machine Learning a family of statistical approaches that use modern statistics to find patterns, classify outcomes, and make predictions using massive amounts of data.
MECE Principle Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive
MLaaS Machine Learning as a Service
P&L Framework Profit & Loss or Income Statement framework helps determine if and how a product could be profitable.
Predictive Approaches attempt to predict a categorical or numeric outcome given a collection of inputs.
Prescriptive Approaches build on descriptive and predictive models with a bias toward action over monitoring.
R&D Research and Development
Rapid Prototyping A group of techniques used to quickly fabricate a scale model of a physical part or assembly using three-dimensional computer-aided design data. In software, it is an approach to software development that emphasizes quick, iterative development cycles and minimal feature sets.
Recommender Systems exploit similarities between cases (such as customer purchase behavior) to recommend a next item or action based on what was preferred by those most similar to the case in hand.
Regency You may have gathered more data recently than in the past for some reason, and that recent data may be skewed for some reason, and not well represent the full variety of data that your ML model will encounter in the future.
Regression Predicting toward a numerical outcome
Reinforcement Learning represents a family of algorithms that learn in a “trial and error” fashion.
Semi-Supervised Learning combines supervised and supervised learning techniques in cases for which only a small number of cases are "labeled" (i.e., have a measured outcome associated with them).
SG&A Selling, General and Administrative
Supervised Learning Methods target a designated outcome, whether it be a numerical value or a category.
Testing Data A partition of data used to evaluate your machine learning model.
Testing Data Subset held out from model training used to test model fit.
Training Data A partition of data used to train your machine learning models.
Training Data Subset used to train the machine learning model.
Training Data Bias When the data being used to train the system is not representative of real data
Unsupervised Learning Models exploit patterns in the data but are not concerned about predicting a specific outcome.
User Layer front-end environment used to develop products and solutions
Value Chain A set of activities that an organization carries out to create value for its customers.
Value Chain Framework A process to determine how to build the most impactful product for the least cost.
Wagile This term refers to teams as they transition from Waterfall to Agile.
Waterfall A project management process that is linear in nature where one phase must be done before moving on the next.
