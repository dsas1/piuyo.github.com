Determining Priority in Chosen Projects - Rack and Stack

Rack and Stack: Summary

Let's say I have a number of projects that I'm considering for inclusion on a data science roadmap, and I'd like to assess each of them on various aspects that reflect their potential on that roadmap.

Let's say the first of these is a propensity model that predicts a customer's likelihood of responding to a marketing offer. Customer purchase behavior is directly aligned with most companies' strategic goals, so we would rate this high in that regard. Typical propensity models use data that is already gathered by the business, and often, this data exists in one or only a few different sources, so the cost of this project will likely be low, and the complexity of implementing this project will likely be low as well. Propensity modeling is one of the more promising areas of marketing; it tends to deliver pretty regularly with relatively large benefits, with the little risk associated with such projects. As such, we would rate this project as having a relatively high certainty of value capture and a relatively large magnitude of benefit.

Let's consider a second project now. Let's say the business has challenges with backlogs in responding to customers' requests. Customers often email or text the company with service questions, but it takes a long time for a human to respond appropriately to those questions. The company would like to look into developing a customer service chatbot that uses natural language processing to automatically assess the need represented by each message and respond to that need automatically. How might we rate the Customer Service Chatbot project?

First, customer service is certainly aligned with strategic goals, so we would rate that as high. The cost of developing a chatbot would be pretty extensive, however; it would take quite a lot of research and tuning to understand categories of customer needs and develop the right responses to be provided automatically. As such we would rate the costs of this project as high. By the same token, the complexity of implementing a system of this kind would be pretty substantial, so we would rate complexity as high as well. Chatbots must be customized to a company's customer needs and developing the right responses can be a speculative exercise, so the certainty of value capture here would be relatively low as well. If the company were able to pull it off, the benefit would be substantial -- the company would increase customer satisfaction through much quicker response times and could do so with lower payroll impact.

Components for assessing candidate data science projects:

  1. Feasibility (time, investment)
  2. Business value impact
  3. Likelihood of value capture

Where do we start? Notes

When choosing a first data science project, consider not only feasibility, impact, and the likelihood of value capture, but also choose cases that will

  • Create the greatest value quickly
  • Generate momentum
  • Foster buy-in for future data science investments
