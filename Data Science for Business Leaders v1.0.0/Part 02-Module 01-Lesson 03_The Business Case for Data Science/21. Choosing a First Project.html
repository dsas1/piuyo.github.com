Choosing a First Project

ND045 DSBL L02 Edge Case - Choosing A First Project

Edge Case: Choosing a first project that is neither greatest in impact nor greatest in feasibility Text

Choosing a First Project Summary

As we discussed during this lesson, when prioritizing data science projects for a roadmap, those projects should be assessed in terms of feasibility, potential impact, and the likelihood of value capture. This is especially critical in the initial stages of data science adoption in order to develop and maintain enthusiasm, momentum, and continued investment in data science from the business.

Reflect

QUESTION:

A leader is deciding which of two candidate projects would be best to implement first in a data science roadmap. The two are similar in cost, time, and complexity of implementation, but project A is projected to bring a 15% greater benefit than project B. In your own words, please provide a reason that the leader might wisely choose to launch the roadmap with project B, even though that project is not likely to bring as great a benefit.

ANSWER:

In addition to feasibility (cost, time, complexity of implementation) and projected impact, projects should be assessed on likelihood of value capture - the likelihood that they deliver their expected benefits. Project B would make a more compelling case than Project A if, in fact, it was much more likely to succeed. This is especially critical in the initial stages of data science adoption in order to develop and maintain enthusiasm, momentum, and continued investment in data science from the business.
