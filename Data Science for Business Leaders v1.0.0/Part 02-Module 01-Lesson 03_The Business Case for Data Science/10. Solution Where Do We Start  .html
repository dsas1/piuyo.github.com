Where Do We Start? Exercise Solution Summary

This exercise had you complete a “Rack and Stack” worksheet for six projects in marketing and procurement & supply chain.
The exercise directed you to determine the relative strategic alignment, cost, complexity of implementation, certainty of value capture, and magnitude of benefit for each of six projects.

  1. Customer loyalty program is directly aligned with strategic goals. Because of the extensive data required, as well as the need to build a system of loyalty offer delivery as part of the project, is only medium in both cost and complexity of implementation. I project large benefits for this project, but because this is the business's first foray into loyalty programs, and many different parts of the business must align in order to execute, the likelihood of initially capturing value is not high.
  2. Customer loyalty assessment is also aligned closely with strategic goals. The departmental edges of the project are much more narrow, so the cost and complexity of implementation are much lower. Action on the information gained is not clearly defined, however, so only a medium benefit is projected. For this reason, as well, the project does not have a high likelihood of value delivery.
  3. Assessing customer attitudes is aligned with strategic goals and has the lowest cost and implementation complexity of the set, as it requires no new data, relying on extant data from social media sites only. Moreover, methods for scraping such data are well-established, which contributes to the high likelihood of capturing value. The benefit of this project is projected to be among the lowest of the group, however, as there is no clear value-driven use identified for the information generated from the project.
  4. Procurement optimization is closely aligned with strategic goals, as Procurement is key to this business. This project will require the purchase of external market data, so cost is moderate, but there will be little need for new data internally, so the complexity of implementation should be relatively low. Raw material procurement is a critical component of the business's operations, so optimizing procurement should bring the medium to high benefit. Material pricing is driven by market factors out of the business's control, however, so there is substantial uncertainty around value capture.
  5. Supply chain optimization is directly aligned with strategic goals, as supply chain operations are also mission-critical to the company. It will require a fair amount of data engineering to bring data from silos associated with the many factories, so the cost will be moderate, but complexity should not be huge once these ETL pipelines are built. Purchasing and inventory are cost-heavy operations, so centralizing and optimizing these functions should bring large benefits to the company. The project's success is incumbent on operational changes in not only procurement and inventory but also delivery, so the project has only medium certainty of delivering on the value expected of it.
  6. Demand forecasting is also closely aligned to strategic goals. This project will rely primarily on established data streams, so both cost and complexity should be relatively low. Being able to anticipate demand swings and schedule production and manage inventory accordingly are important levers to the business, so the project should bring a medium to high benefit to the business. Moreover, demand forecasting methods are well-established, so this project should be among the most likely to deliver on its expected value.
