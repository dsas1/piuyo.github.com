03. Welcome to the Data Science for Business Leaders Program

# Welcome to the Data Science for Business Leaders Executive Program!

How will you benefit from this Nanodegree program?

The volume of data that exists in our world is growing exponentially, with analysis suggesting that 90% of all data was created in just the past couple years. McKinsey Global Institute reports that data-driven organizations are now 23 times more likely to acquire customers, 6 times as likely to retain customers, and 19 times as likely to be profitable as a result. To succeed in this era, business leaders need to understand how to create and implement strategies that harness the power of big data. With Udacity’s new Data Science for Business Leaders Executive Program, you’ll learn how to lead an organization’s data science strategy to achieve transformational business outcomes. You’ll evaluate best practices for how to structure your data science team, promote a data-driven culture, and prepare detailed Data and Data Architecture strategies for your business. With a real-world, hands-on project, you will Identify and prioritize data science opportunities, build the technical and cultural infrastructure of a data science team, and determine the optimal Data and Machine Learning system architectures to enable data science initiatives across an organization.

What the program offers

In this course, you will acquire the necessary skills to advance your career as a business leader in today’s data-driven workplace through hands-on practice prioritizing, scoping, and facilitating data initiatives within an enterprise. You'll learn to:

  1. Classify data science projects in terms of Area, Approach, and Type of Model
  2. For a given Area, Approach, and Model Type, provide one example project from your business
  3. Given the particulars of a data science project, identify areas of concern that might lead to the projects failing.
  4. Given the particulars of a data science project, identify steps that could be taken to help ensure the project succeeds
  5. Define an organization’s data science roadmap
  6. Identify the best projects(s) to start with
  7. Detail strategies for successfully launching data science initiatives
  8. Determine a starting point -- the most appropriate first project (or suite of projects) to capture the most promising opportunities and launch the data science function with adequate momentum to ensure its long-term operation within the organization.
  9. Work with fellow executives to set and manage reasonable expectations of success for data science projects
  10. Given a set of candidate data science projects, determine the relative strategic importance, cost, complexity of implementation, risk, likelihood of value capture, and magnitude of benefit for each of the five projects
  11. For any data science project, identify strategies for meeting three key factors of success (executive sponsorship; strategic alignment with core business interests; scope conditions)
  12. Use the Data Science Heat Map as a tool for specifying roles within the Data Science organization
  13. Manage Data Science operations using structured processes for work and communication
  14. Given the particulars of a company’s strategic and operating contexts, identify the data science organizational model best suited for that company.
  15. Given a data science strategy, identify and prioritize the mix of roles you would pursue to build out the data science organization.
  16. Describe the project and product management strategies best suited for a given company’s data science organizations
  17. Given a broad business challenge, describe how you would approach the development of a data science strategy using the Structured Problem Solving Method.
  18. Given a business context, identify strategies for promoting a data-driven culture throughout that business, particularly, around guiding employees on how to think through breaking down problems of identifying data consumers, data needs/use cases, data sources, and related necessary pipeline/ transformations that need to happen
  19. Given a particular business context, prepare a detailed Data and Data Architecture strategy
  20. Given a particular business context, detail how a Machine Learning Architecture strategy fits into its Data and Data Architecture strategy.
  21. Identify the strengths and weaknesses of a given business’s Data and Data Architecture strategy

Throughout the program, you will work with:

  • Interactive lesson content
  • Exercises
  • Hands-on project where you will apply and practice your new skills.
  • Professional review of your project by our project reviewers who will provide personalized feedback on how you can improve your work.

These projects will serve as a portfolio you can use to demonstrate your skills. After you complete the program, you will be ready to lead a business organization or function to execute transformational data initiatives or bring these crucial skills to your current role.

Almost every executive role in today’s workforce, particularly at enterprise technology companies, will require working with data and we are excited to support you in this step in your career.

We are excited and honored that you are taking this step in your journey with us!
