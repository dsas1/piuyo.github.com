19. Quizzes: Why Data Science Initiatives Fail

Which of the following represents common reasons for the failure of data science initiatives?
SOLUTION:
  • The project didn’t produce the results expected by the business.
  • Data required of the initiative exists in different silos and could not be integrated or reconciled.
  • Potential ethical, social, and regulatory issues were not identified.
  • The project failed to maintain sufficient support from the leadership of the organization.
  • Expectations were set too high and the resultant product doesn’t measure up to them.

Reflect

QUESTION:

In order to "sell" the executive team on a data science initiative, a leader has strongly promoted all the exciting things that data science will do to transform the business. What is one risk inherent in this strategy for gaining executive buy-in?

ANSWER:

The temptation to promote the potential of data science is natural, but there can be many risks in 'overselling' data science projects. One primary risk that often leads to failure of data science projects occurs when expectations are set too high and the resultant product fails to measure up.
